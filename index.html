Establishment & History

How Communest Came to Be

Communest was conceived in 2024 by Kipngeno Shammah Kiplangat, a student at Pioneer International University pursuing a Bachelor of Information Technology, under the supervision of Dr. Odoyo. The platform emerged from a clear need observed across Nairobi's rapidly growing residential estate sector.

Estate managers were drowning in paper-based systems — handwritten rent receipts, physical complaint books, and phone call chains for announcements. Tenants had no central place to track their lease, payments, or submit issues. The digital divide between management and residents was costing time, money, and trust.

Communest was built to close that gap: a single digital space where an estate can exist online, where outsiders can browse and apply, and where tenants can manage their residency transparently.

📅

🖥️

🚀

Communest goes live with 6 seed estates, full tenant management, photo galleries, and public inquiry features.

Legal & Compliance
📜

Title Deeds

All estates listed on Communest are encouraged to register their title deed reference numbers for transparency. These are displayed on the estate's About page for prospective tenants to verify ownership through the Lands Registry.

🏛️

Land Registration Act, 2012

Communest operates in alignment with Kenya's Land Registration Act, 2012, which governs ownership, transfer, and leasing of land and residential property across the country.

🤝

Tenancy Agreements

Management teams are required to maintain formal lease agreements with all tenants. Communest stores lease start and end dates, monthly rent, and unit details digitally.

🔒

Data Privacy

Tenant data is scoped strictly to their estate. No personal information is shared across estates or made public. Management can only access data for their own registered estate.

How It Works

Simple for Everyone

Whether you're looking for a home, managing an estate, or already a tenant — Communest has a clear flow for you.

🏙️

1. Browse

Explore all listed estates, filter by location, view photos, and browse available houses at your own pace — no sign-in required.

📋

2. Apply

Found a house you like? Submit an application directly. The estate's management team reviews it and assigns you a unit upon approval.

🏠

3. Manage

Once connected, sign in to your estate's dashboard to pay rent, submit requests, view announcements, and manage your lease — all in one place.

Academic Context

A Final-Year Project with Real-World Impact

Communest is submitted as a final-year project for the Bachelor of Information Technology degree at Pioneer International University, Nairobi. The project addresses real systemic issues in Kenya's residential property management sector.

The platform demonstrates full-stack web development capabilities: multi-user authentication, role-based access control, estate-scoped data isolation, file uploads for photo galleries, and a responsive mobile-first design — all built without backend infrastructure, using the browser as a runtime environment.

The goal was to produce not just a functional prototype, but a deployable product that could genuinely serve Kenyan estate managers and their tenants from day one.
